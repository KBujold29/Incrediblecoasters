Medusa Review

We're here at Six Flags Discovory Kingdom. Today's ride we'll be reviewing for you is Medusa. Say goodbye to the floor, and away you go! After a small dip, you go straight to the lifthill. And then you climb. Eventually, you reach the top. Then you go through a small turn before you drop. The drop on Medusa is fantastic! Down and down you rush. It's all one drop. No twisty bits on Medusa. It's straight. Overall, I'd say that Medusa has the best drop out of all the floorless coasters I've been on. And then you soar up into a loop. Up and around 360 degrees. You get some nice hangtime in the loop, but at the bottom, you're traveling back at your original speed, but then you soar up into a dive loop. Once you've reached the point of going upsidedown, you then level back out and regain your speed. And then what's coming up is the best part of the ride. The part that makes even the toughest man giggle like a schoolgirl. The Heartline Spin! The force of the heartline spin just twirls us and gives a ride before bringing you back to ground level. And then it's off into a sea serpent roll. This is unique as it is the only B&M coaster to have a sea serpent roll (Though it's on SLCs around the world). Anyways, it's MUCH better on Medusa. Though to be honest, I prefer cobra rolls. Then you twist up and head for the brake run. From here, you head into a big diving curvy drop. This drop is simply awesome and gives us quite a bit of speed. We then whip into a corkscrew. Now the corkscrew on Medusa is a bigass corkscrew. Then we roll through a sort of helixy turn. We roar through this and provides us with some laterals. You then go through another awesome corkscrew before you roar up through a helix. This helix is awesome and has many forces. Sadly, we then glide into the brake run. Though Dominator is better, Medusa is a fantastic floorless coaster I would totally recommend this if you're at Six Flags Discovory Kingdom.

8/10

Location: Six Flags Discovory Kingdom

Opened: 2000

Built by: B&M

Last Ridden: November 3, 2019
